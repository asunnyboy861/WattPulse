## Pricing Strategy Rationale

### Why Paid Download (Not Subscription)

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **No ongoing costs** | Core ML on-device inference + Home Assistant local data = zero server/API costs |
| **Target user profile** | Solar/smart home users who already invested $5K-$30K in equipment; $4.99 is insignificant |
| **Lower decision barrier** | One-time purchase avoids subscription fatigue; users hate yet another monthly fee |
| **Competitive advantage** | Competitors are free but weak; $4.99 delivers far superior value |
| **App Store category fit** | $4.99 is the "sweet spot" for Utilities paid apps — above $0.99 (cheap feel), below $9.99 (hesitation) |

### Pricing Psychology

| Principle | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Anchoring** | Sense costs $349 hardware; WattPulse $4.99 = 1.4% of that → perceived as "great value" |
| **Psychological pricing** | $4.99 < $5.00 → below consumer mental threshold → easier decision |
| **Coffee comparison** | $4.99 = one Starbucks coffee → easy to rationalize purchase |
| **Equipment investment** | Solar system $15K-$30K; $4.99 = 0.03% additional investment → negligible |
| **ROI perception** | Saves $30-100/month on electricity; $4.99 one-time → extremely high ROI |
