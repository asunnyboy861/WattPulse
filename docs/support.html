WattPulse Support

Welcome to WattPulse support. We're here to help you get the most out of your home energy monitoring.

Getting Started

  1. Install WattPulse from the App Store.
  2. Open the app and follow the onboarding flow.
  3. Enter your Home Assistant URL (e.g., http://homeassistant.local:8123).
  4. Create a Long-Lived Access Token in Home Assistant: Profile → Long-Lived Access Tokens → Create Token.
  5. Paste the token into WattPulse and tap Authorize.
  6. Review the discovered energy entities and tap Start Monitoring.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I create a Home Assistant Long-Lived Access Token?

A: Open Home Assistant in a web browser. Click your profile picture (bottom left). Scroll to "Long-Lived Access Tokens" at the bottom. Click "Create Token", give it a name like "WattPulse", and copy the token. Paste it into WattPulse during setup.

Q: Why can't WattPulse connect to Home Assistant?

A: Common causes:
1. Wrong URL — make sure to include http:// or https:// and the port (usually :8123).
2. Network — your iPhone must be on the same network as Home Assistant, or use a VPN.
3. Token expired — create a new Long-Lived Access Token in HA.
4. HA version — WattPulse requires Home Assistant 2023.x or later.

Q: Why are no energy entities discovered?

A: WattPulse looks for sensors with specific patterns (e.g., "solar", "grid", "battery", "power"). If your HA uses custom names, you may need to rename sensors or add them manually in Settings. Make sure your HA has the Energy dashboard configured.

Q: How are costs calculated?

A: Go to Settings → Electricity Rate Plan and choose:
- Flat Rate: single $/kWh value
- Time of Use: peak/shoulder/off-peak rates with hour ranges
- Real-Time Pricing: uses market data when available from HA

Q: How do I export my energy data?

A: Go to Settings → Data Export → choose a time range → tap "Export CSV". The file can be shared via the iOS share sheet.

Q: How does the AI suggestion engine work?

A: WattPulse analyzes your historical energy patterns on-device (no cloud) and generates suggestions like "Run dishwasher during solar peak" or "Battery low — consider charging from grid during off-peak hours". Suggestions appear on the Dashboard and Insights tabs.

Q: What is anomaly detection?

A: WattPulse learns your typical usage patterns and alerts you when consumption is 2.5x higher than normal. You can mark alerts as "normal" to improve detection accuracy.

Q: Is my data sent to the cloud?

A: No. All energy data, cost calculations, and AI suggestions are processed and stored locally on your device. See our Privacy Policy.

Q: Can I monitor multiple Home Assistant instances?

A: Yes. Go to Settings → Home Assistant → "Add Another HA Instance". You can switch between instances by tapping one in the list.

Q: Why does the app show "Offline" sometimes?

A: WattPulse shows cached data when the connection to Home Assistant is lost. The banner at the top of the Dashboard indicates offline status. Data will refresh automatically when the connection is restored.

Troubleshooting

  • App crashes on launch: Force-quit and reopen. If persistent, reinstall the app (your HA settings are stored in Keychain and will persist).
  • Charts show no data: Verify HA is running and energy sensors are reporting. Try changing the time range in the Dashboard.
  • Costs show $0: Configure a rate plan in Settings → Electricity Rate Plan.
  • Notifications not working: Enable notifications in iOS Settings → WattPulse, and in the app's Settings tab.
